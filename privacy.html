Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 25, 2026

FnMark is built with privacy as a first principle. This policy explains exactly what data we collect — which is nothing.

What We Collect

Nothing. FnMark runs entirely on your Mac. It has no analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporting, no remote logging, and no internet connection of any kind.

Data Stored on Your Device

FnMark stores the following locally on your Mac, never leaving your device:

  • Your settings (toolbar opacity, favorite colors, keyboard shortcuts, cursor preferences)
  • Your last annotation session (if Save Session is enabled)

This data is stored using macOS UserDefaults inside the FnMark app sandbox. You can remove all of it by uninstalling the app.

Permissions

FnMark may request the following macOS permissions:

  • Screen Recording — used by the Zoom Lens and Export-as-PNG features. Required to capture pixels from your display. The captured image is processed entirely on-device and never transmitted.
  • Accessibility — used to detect global hotkeys (such as the Fn key) so you can toggle annotation from any app.

You can revoke these at any time in System Settings → Privacy & Security.

Third Parties

FnMark does not integrate with any third-party services, SDKs, ad networks, or analytics providers.

Children's Privacy

FnMark does not collect data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date revised.
